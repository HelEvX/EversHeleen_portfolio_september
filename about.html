Hello! I'm Heleen

Digital Designer & Videographer | based in Hasselt, Belgium

When I went to university in Leuven (KUL) I just wanted to indulge myself. I chose archaeology, to me the perfect blend of art, history & science. I spent a lot of my time examining shards, tombs, knapped flints and landscapes before deciding to dig elsewhere.

I had the urge to learn a technical skill with a hint of artistry, so I took a videography course at Syntra Genk. I adored it, but at the time it didn't click just yet and I kept on searching. After excursions into food sciences and teaching, the lockdown of 2020 finally gave me the space I needed to revisit editing - with motion graphics thrown into the mix for the sheer fun of it.

I'm proud of my academic streak and quirky tastes. I've worked on film sets and theatre stages, in snack bars and hospitals, industrial labs and care home kitchens.

Currently I'm focusing on digital design at PXL Hasselt and several online courses on the side. I'm thrilled about exploring new areas such as user experience research and sharpening my graphic skills.



Here are three more things I'd like to share.

  • I have synaesthesia (my favourite colour is 24).
  • I can fold origami dinosaurs from memory.
  • I have my own research microscope and a lab coat.

My personal branding is inspired by red-figure pottery, nutrient agar petri dishes and fossils trapped in amber. Because my past obsessions shape me to this day!

What Do I Do?

I've experimented with: logo animations, title sequences, kinetic type, trailers, after-movies, corporate videos & multi camera live capturing. A few of my pet projects are online collaborations of which I'm immensely proud!

My proudest moment is being invited by my favourite comedians to create animated end cards for their YouTube Channel after they spotted my very first motion graphic experiment, which I had entered as part of a fan art challenge.
